================================================================================
NEIGHBOURHOOD EUI ANALYSIS: 24 vs 48 BUILDINGS
================================================================================

OBSERVATION:
------------
NUs_RC1 (24 Buildings): Heating EUI = 73.6 kWh/m²
NUs_RC2 (48 Buildings): Heating EUI = 97.0 kWh/m² (+32% Increase)

WHY IS HEATING HIGHER IN THE DENSER NEIGHBOURHOOD?
--------------------------------------------------
The primary cause is REDUCED PASSIVE SOLAR GAINS due to MUTUAL SHADING.

1. BENEFICIAL SOLAR GAIN (The "Free Heat" Effect)
   - In winter, low-angle sunlight enters windows and provides significant heating.
   - For detached houses with good glazing, this can offset 20-40% of heating load.

2. THE "URBAN CANYON" EFFECT IN RC2
   - RC2 doubles the density (48 vs 24 buildings).
   - Buildings are likely placed closer together or in additional rows.
   - Front rows cast shadows on back rows.
   - Side-by-side buildings block morning/afternoon sun.

3. IMPACT ON ENERGY BALANCE
   - RC1: Buildings are spaced enough to receive full solar access.
   - RC2: Many facades define "self-shading" or "mutual shading" obstructions.
   - Result: The heating system must compensate for the lost solar heat.

CONCLUSION:
-----------
The 32% increase in heating EUI confirms that the densification in RC2 
significantly compromises solar access. This is a realistic physical 
phenomenon in urban energy modeling.
